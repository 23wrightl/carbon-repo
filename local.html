Taking the Leap
Jane Spelman
Published 12/6/2021

How might companies and/or organizations influence the progress a community would make towards a cleaner environment and decreased carbon footprint?

 Companies and large-scale organizations have the largest impacts on an areas’ or communities’ carbon emissions. They create and dispose of the most products and have a large influence on the environment. To be honest, there is not much individuals can do if there are immense amounts of waste and carbon emissions being produced by local corporations. Therefore, the best thing an individual can do for their community is work with others to decrease the carbon emissions created by companies and organizations.
 But what can a single person do that would convince companies to change their ways to a more environmentally friendly but possibly more immediately costly method of production? Becoming more active politically by using your voice and vote is a great way to fight for a more sustainable future. You can also turn to more environmentally conscious companies for products. There are also plenty of nonprofits that you can help out with donations or purchases.


 A few environmental nonprofits that support climate justice are Cool Effect, who strive to reduce carbon emissions, and Project Drawdown, who are working to lay out solutions for reversing global warming. These are only a couple of many groups of people working for a cleaner and healthier world.
