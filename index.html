Introduction

Carbon Footprint

[kahr-buhn foot-print]

Your carbon footprint is the amount of greenhouse gases that are released by your own actions. Greenhouse gases are gases found in Earth's atmosphere that trap heat which then leads to increased planet heating and the greenhouse effect or climate change.

Adult Resources


Learning

Biggest Producers of Carbon Emissions

We all know that we release a lot of carbon when we run drive our cars. But what else produces carbon emissions? And what can we do to reduce it?

What Can You Do to Reduce Your Carbon Footprint at Home?

We understand that life is busy and you can't always join a big campaign or work with these huge organizations to make world-wide change. So, here is some simple changes at home you can make.

Using Your Voice Through Voting

If you are the more ambitious type, you can go the extra mile and work with your local government to create change. Even something as simple as voting for a clean energy bill goes a long way!

How Can Travelling Hurt the Environment Around You?

We all take nice vacations to the beach or the mountains, but have you ever considered how your roadtrip or quick flight to get there is hurting the environment?

Dieting: Not Just for Your Health

Trying to eat healthier is better for your body, and better for the environment. Shopping local and organic is a big step in reducing our carbon emissions.

Local Organizations: Taking the Leap

There are many activism groups out there to work to help reduce the world's carbon footprint. Maybe it is worthwhile to join one too?

Little Ripples in a Big Pond

Not working to help our environment is common because some feel that their impact is too little to count. But, if we do nothing at all we will watch as our home crumbles around us; let's change that mindset.
