Collecting Rainwater: Let's Not Make a Big Splash
Afton Turner
Published: 12/8/2021

Recap: Carbon emissions is the amount of carbon dioxide (a type of gas) that is released into our atmosphere. Our atmosphere is the protective bubble that surrounds our planet. When we put too many of those greenhouse gases into the atmosphere, we can heat up the planet, and that is so harmful to nature!



Water Production
 Letting the sink continue running or taking too long of showers can waste water. Why? Because 97% of water on the Earth is salt water! So we can't drink it!
 The water we do drink comes from lakes, underground pools of water, rivers, and a lot of other freshwater resources! This water gets cleaned and filtered, which gets rid of all the bacteria and bad particles that would make us sick before it reaches the tap.
 The materials, machines, and other resources required to purify and collect the water you drink, water your garden with, and clean with is bad for the environment. The garbage and waste that’s leftover from all the equipment pollutes the land!

Fun Fact
Acid rain is rain that has collected pollution, dust, and other harmful things on its way down here. Make sure to get a filter and purification system for your rainwater! Acid rain can contaminate your rainwater supply if you’re not careful!


Rainwater Harvesting
 Rainwater harvesting is when you collect rainwater from roof overflow and put it into a storage container.
 This rainwater can be used for things like washing, cleaning, bathing, and cooking! Rainwater is also better for your garden than the water we drink because some of the chemicals we can’t drink are actually good for plants! And some of the purification chemicals we can drink are actually bad for plants! So you can use your collected rainwater to water your plants or make a little vegetable garden!

There are a few ways you can collect rainwater. You can set out a few buckets before it rains and then bring them back inside once they’re full, or you can set up a collection system like this! There are tubes that guide the water into a giant tank and cleans it so you can use it!

Did you know that 2 U.S. states don’t let you drink the rainwater you collect? And 10 have rules you have to follow to collect rainwater! Crazy right?
