The Digital Mining
Afton Turner
Published 12/1/2021

Ethereum, Ripple XRP, Litecoin, NEO, IOTA, and the ever-famous Bitcoin.
Many names, many brands, all encompassed under one thing: Cryptocurrency.

 Cryptocurrency has drastically risen in popularity in the past ten years, and for many, it is a source of pocket money or even income. It has become famous not only in technology and finance circles but around the entire world.
 People willing to invest their money in crypto have multiplied, seemingly ten-fold. People are rushing to buy before prices rise too high; crypto is the supposed "currency of the future" after all. Over 18,000 businesses already accept some form of cryptocurrency as an alternative to cash or card payment. Bitcoin has risen by 311,914% in value between 2012 and November of 2021!
 Speaking of Bitcoin: did you know it has a highly negative impact on the environment?

How Bitcoin Mining Works

 Before we get into the nitty-gritty part of crypto mining’s environmental impact, let's explore what, exactly, mining is:

"New bitcoins are released through mining, which is actually the process of validating and recording new transactions in the blockchain. The miner who achieves this first is rewarded with new bitcoin."

- Columbia Climate School

 Mining Bitcoin is not like literal mining. You don't need a pickaxe and a hard hat with a headlight. All you need to build a basic mining rig is a motherboard, graphics card (or GPUS, short for Graphics Processing Units), a power supply, some form of a cooling unit, a CPU, and a frame to hold it all in.

Graphic from bitpanda.com

You can find more information on how to build and operate a mining rig at: Learn How To Build A Mining Rig: Things To Know Before The Start

Bit-Mining's Nasty Byte

Unbeknownst to non-miners, Bit-Mining uses alarming amounts of energy and electricity.


The high energy usage from Bit-Mining is tremendously high; around 35.95 million tons of carbon dioxide can be attributed to Bit-Mining every year. That's the same amount of carbon dioxide emissions the entire country of New Zealand emits in a year.
