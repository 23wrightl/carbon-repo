Alternative Energy Sources: Power Your Future
Afton Turner
Published: 12/8/2021

Recap: Carbon emissions is the amount of carbon dioxide (a type of gas) that is released into our atmosphere. Our atmosphere is the protective bubble that surrounds our planet. When we put too many of those greenhouse gases into the atmosphere, we can heat up the planet, and that is so harmful to nature!


Solar Power
 Solar power is power from the sun! It’s super useful because it doesn’t have any side-effects, like pollution or smoke. Instead, it uses the sun’s energy and rays and turns those into energy! We then use that energy and turn it into electricity, which can be used to power houses, buildings, cars, and so much more! You can even put some on the roof of your house to help power it!
 But, it’s not perfect. Only a small amount of the sunlight solar panels soak up can be used to create electricity! Most people that use solar power have too many solar panels to put on their roof, so huge amounts of forests and fields are used to put these solar panels in, which can be bad for the environment.

Wind Power
 Wind power is power from the wind. It is not the best energy source because the wind turbines that we use to collect energy take up a lot of space and can be easily broken. Since wind turbines are so big and fragile, they break a lot because of all the wind. These giant broken parts are thrown away to landfills and are like huge pieces of metal garbage. Not very good for the environment! They’re also super high up, so sometimes birds will fly into them!
 However, wind energy doesn’t give off smoke or put dangerous chemicals into the air! So it’s better than fossil fuels like coal and gas in that sense.

Rainwater Harvesting
 Nuclear power sounds scary at first because of something else called a nuclear bomb. Nuclear bombs are super dangerous weapons that get their destructive power from the energy that holds an atom together: nuclear energy. Nuclear power does the same thing! Except, it isn’t made to explode and destroy things.
 Nuclear power is created at something called a nuclear power plant, which is a huge facility with lots of giant buildings! Inside those giant buildings is something called Uranium, a mineral we can dig up from underground. The Uranium gets super hot and mixes with water which creates steam. The steam rises up and spins turbines at the top of the buildings and that creates energy! This also means that the only thing nuclear power plants put into the air is water vapor, which is actually good!

 All that white stuff is water vapor!

 It can be risky though. If something in the buildings isn’t designed right or an engineer isn’t paying attention, the nuclear power plant can explode! It has only happened three times in all of history, so it’s very rare. There’s also a side-product from nuclear power plants called nuclear waste! There isn’t a lot of it, but it’s bad for the environment. But! Scientists are studying nuclear waste and finding ways to use it as fuel for nuclear power plants! Reduce, reuse, recycle!
