Let's Take a Tour of a Farm!
Lainey Wright and Jane Spelman
Published:11/01/2021

Recap: Carbon emissions is the amount of carbon dioxide (a type of gas) that is released into our atmosphere. Our atmosphere is the protective bubble that surrounds our planet. When we put too many of those greenhouse gases into the atmosphere, we can heat up the planet, and that is so harmful to nature!

Cows
Bigger animals, like cows, need a lot of water to drink. And creating freshwater requires a lot of energy because 97% of water on Earth is salt water! Cows can’t drink salt water and neither can humans! Also, cows need grassy fields to graze, so farmers need to use freshwater to water their fields. Vegetables and other edible plants don’t need as much water and they don’t need it as often so they have a smaller water footprint.

Fun Fact: 1,847 gallons of water are used to make 1 pound of beef
(That’s 39 bathtubs full of water!)


Pigs
Farms produce a lot of carbon emissions, and pigs are no different. When animals breathe out, they breathe out carbon dioxide (the bad gas for our atmosphere). Pigs breathe out a lot of that gas and having a ton of pigs on a huge farm can increase the amount of carbon dioxide! Also, the manure (poop) that pigs release has a lot of CO2!

Fun Fact: 40 ounces of carbon dioxide is released when making 4 ounces of pork


Chickens
Growing chicken feed needs fertilizers, which make a lot of CO2 when spreading it across the growing fields. Also, the meat from chickens makes a lot of different types of food, that all need water to make them. And, from when we learned about cows, we know that using a lot of water is not good for our environment!

Fun Fact: Chicken production makes up to 404 pounds of CO2 each year


All of this really hurts our environment, but how can we help?

What can we do to help conserve water?
  • Eat less red meat
  • Take shorter showers and less baths
  • Don’t leave water running when its not in use
  • Fix leaky faucets right when they start leaking
What can we do to help conserve our land?
  • Save space by reusing buildings and already developed areas that are no longer in use
  • Build things that are multipurpose

The best solution: Just eat less meat! These farms have been really hurting our environment, and eating less of what they make can help!
