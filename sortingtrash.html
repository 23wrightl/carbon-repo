How Does This Help Our Carbon Footprint?
Lainey Wright
Published: 11/08/2021

Recap: Carbon emissions is the amount of carbon dioxide (a type of gas) that is released into our atmosphere. Our atmosphere is the protective bubble that surrounds our planet. When we put too many of those greenhouse gases into the atmosphere, we can heat up the planet, and that is so harmful to nature!

Do you have two big trash cans in your garage? Or maybe it's sitting next to your house? Is one of them a different color than the other? It is possible that the different one is not a trash can at all, but rather a recycling bin! And we put special items such as plastic and cardboard in there because they can be reused! Let’s see how!


Step 1: The House
All of our waste starts somewhere, our house! You use things all the time, like the little bags that hold your snacks, or the boxes that hold your toys. Everything has a package that it is stored in and they go into your trash cans. But what do we do with that waste?


Step 2: The Sorting
When we are finished with something and want to throw it away, we can sort it into different cans. The first one is regular trash. This includes food-related items such as granola bar wrappers and chip bags, some paper items like stickers and tissues, and other random items such as bubble wrap and disposable gloves. Most of our trash goes into the trash bin.

The second bin that they could go into is the recycling bin! This includes most plastics like plastic bins and drink bottles and paper products like newspapers and cardboard. You are allowed to recycle an item if it has the logo on the bottom with a number of 1 through 7 in the middle of it.

The last bin that we have in the US is the composting bin. Some schools have worked to provide access to them through their environment clubs. These are normally green with the word “composting” across the side. You can put any food inside of these such as your banana peel or leftovers from lunch.


Step 3: The Transportation
About once a week, a garbage truck will come to pick up all the trash in your bins! They will take the trash to the landfill, a huge space where we dump all our trash, or they will take it to a recycling center, where the plastics and paper will be reused as other items. These trucks use large “arms” to pick up the trash and dump them into the huge containers they have.


How does this affect your carbon footprint?
Carbon emissions are released through the process of creating these items. The emissions released are far greater when you have to continue to keep creating more instead of reusing it. So by recycling, you cut down your carbon footprint. This infographic shows that process really well!



What can you do?
  • Buy products that are recyclable
  • Sort your trash into a garbage bin and recycling bin
  • Don’t litter
  • Compost your food
